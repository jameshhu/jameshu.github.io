## About

I am a Graduate Research Assistant at the **Quantum Engineering Lab** (led by Prof. Zheshen Zhang) at the **University of Michigan, Ann Arbor**, pursuing my M.S. in Electrical and Computer Engineering (GPA: 4.0/4.0).

My research interests lie in **quantum information**, **photonic chip design**, and **variational quantum algorithms**. The main theme of my work is bridging software optimization with experimental atomic physics. Currently, I focus on conducting high-fidelity photonics and quantum chip simulations using **Tidy3D** to optimize device performance, as well as managing the full hardware testing pipeline from setup to data acquisition.

Previously, I was a Research Assistant at the Institute of Quantum Electronics at **Peking University** working with Prof. Jingbiao Chen on 420 nm-pumped hot-rubidium vapor experiments. I also spent time as a Research Intern in Prof. Xiao Yuan's group at PKU, developing **Variational Quantum Algorithms (VQA)** and neural-network quantum states using **PyTorch**. I obtained my B.E. in Computer Science from **Pennsylvania State University**.
